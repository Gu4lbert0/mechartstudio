Project Summary

This project demonstrates the use of drone-based photogrammetry to create accurate digital representations of real-world environments. Using autonomous flight-planning and photogrammetry software, aerial imagery was transformed into detailed 3D models of a construction site, a section of Yosemite Lake, and a natural river corridor for visualization, documentation, and analysis.

The project emphasized careful image collection, reconstruction quality, scale awareness, and preparing outputs that could be reviewed or used as a reference for design and planning work.

My Role

  • Planned autonomous flight missions using the Litchi app and waypoint-based mapping.
  • Captured high-resolution aerial imagery with the DJI Mini 2.
  • Processed image datasets into detailed 3D models, orthomosaic maps, and terrain reconstructions.
  • Optimized flight paths and image overlap using the Litchi app to improve reconstruction accuracy.
  • Generated digital models of a construction site, a section of Yosemite Lake, and a natural river corridor.
