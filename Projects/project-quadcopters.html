Quadcopter R&D

Research and development focused on autonomous quadcopter systems, custom hardware integration, and experimental propulsion concepts to evaluate new technologies for future robotic aerial platforms.

Back to Projects

Project Summary

The Quadcopter R&D project consists of two ongoing research efforts centered around autonomous flight and experimental aerial vehicle development. The primary objective is to develop a modular research platform that can be used to teach the fundamentals of quadcopter design, system integration, flight control, and autonomous programming. The first project investigated whether a commercial DJI Inspire airframe could be successfully retrofitted with an open-source ArduPilot flight controller to create a stable autonomous research platform. The second project explores the development of a custom hub-style motor and propeller system designed to evaluate the feasibility, physics, and flight characteristics of an alternative propulsion concept for small unmanned aerial vehicles.

My Role

  • Repaired a damaged DJI Inspire airframe by restoring the carbon fiber frame and manufacturing replacement structural attachments.
  • Retrofitted the DJI Inspire with a MicoAir H743 flight controller compatible with ArduPilot for autonomous flight research.
  • Replaced the original propulsion system with D4215 650KV brushless motors and designed custom mounting hardware to integrate the motors into the existing airframe.
  • Designed and assembled the complete electrical system, including wiring harnesses, power distribution, and flight electronics.
  • Designed and manufactured custom replacement and mounting components using a Bambu Lab X1 Carbon 3D printer.
  • Developed safe flight-testing procedures by utilizing a netted trampoline test stand and programming a transmitter kill switch to safely evaluate vehicle stability before unrestricted flight.
  • Designed and developed a prototype hub-style motor and propeller system to investigate an alternative propulsion architecture for small quadcopters.
  • Conducted ongoing research and iterative prototyping to evaluate the performance and feasibility of the experimental propulsion system.
