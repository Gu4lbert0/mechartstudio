Architectural 3D Modeling

Architectural 3D modeling project focused on converting blueprint specifications into detailed digital models using Autodesk Inventor. The project supported architectural visualization through both digital deliverables and physical 3D-printed prototypes.

Back to Projects

Project Summary

During my time at Otto Rigan Studio, I developed three-dimensional architectural models based on client-provided blueprint specifications. Using Autodesk Inventor, I translated two-dimensional drawings into accurate digital representations that could be used for visualization, design review, and project planning.

In addition to creating the digital models, I produced physical prototypes using 3D printing technology, allowing designs to be reviewed and evaluated in a tangible format. The project required careful attention to detail, strong blueprint interpretation skills, and the ability to deliver accurate models within tight project schedules.

The completed work was delivered ahead of schedule, enabling the client to move forward with subsequent phases of development without delays.

My Role

  • Created detailed architectural 3D models in Autodesk Inventor from blueprint specifications.
  • Interpreted and translated 2D drawings into accurate three-dimensional digital models.
  • Prepared models for additive manufacturing and produced physical prototypes using 3D printing.
  • Verified model accuracy against design documentation and client requirements.
  • Managed project deliverables and completed work ahead of schedule..
