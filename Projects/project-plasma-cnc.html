Plasma CNC

An open-source CNC plasma cutting system designed around 4020 aluminum extrusion, with a focus on affordability, ease of assembly, and safe operation for builders and makers.

Back to Projects

Project Summary

The CNC Plasma Cutter project is an ongoing effort to develop a modular plasma cutting system that can be easily assembled using standard 4020 aluminum extrusion and commercially available components. The goal is to create a reliable, cost-effective platform that is straightforward to build while providing comprehensive documentation to simplify assembly and commissioning. Current development focuses on completing the mechanical CAD model, electrical wiring diagrams, and control system architecture. Safety remains a primary design objective, with continuous improvements being made to the mechanical, electrical, and control systems to help users safely construct and operate their own version of the machine.

My Role

  • Designed the mechanical structure of the CNC plasma cutter using modular 4020 aluminum extrusion.
  • Developed the complete CAD assembly and continuously refined the design through iterative prototyping.
  • Designed the electrical architecture and wiring diagrams for the motion control and power distribution systems.
  • Integrated motion control hardware, stepper motors, drivers, and CNC electronics into a unified control system.
  • Developed safety features and electrical protection strategies to improve user safety during assembly and machine operation.
  • Performed system integration, calibration, and testing throughout development.
  • Created technical documentation to support fabrication, assembly, wiring, and future system improvements.
